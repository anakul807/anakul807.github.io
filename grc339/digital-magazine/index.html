Nintendo Entertainment System: The Console That Started It All

Launched in 1985, the Nintendo Entertainment System brought gaming into the living room and redefined the industry after the 1983 crash. Its iconic controller and 8-bit classics like Super Mario Bros. and The Legend of Zelda built a foundation that still resonates with fans today.

Super Nintendo: 16-Bit Legends

Released in 1991, the SNES elevated gaming with vibrant graphics and timeless titles like Super Metroid, Donkey Kong Country, and Chrono Trigger. It solidified Nintendo as a household name worldwide.

Nintendo 64: Entering 3D Worlds

The N64, launched in 1996, introduced players to immersive 3D experiences with games like Super Mario 64 and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, shaping the future of 3D gaming.

GameCube: Compact but Mighty

The 2001 GameCube may have been small in size, but it delivered unforgettable experiences with titles like Super Smash Bros. Melee, Metroid Prime, and Animal Crossing.

Wii: Gaming for Everyone

In 2006, the Wii revolutionized gaming with motion controls, attracting casual players and families with Wii Sports and Mario Kart Wii. It became one of Nintendo’s best-selling consoles.

Wii U: An Overlooked Pioneer

Released in 2012, the Wii U experimented with a tablet-style controller and laid the groundwork for the Switch. While not a commercial success, it hosted gems like Super Mario 3D World and Splatoon.

Handheld Icons: Game Boy

The 1989 Game Boy made gaming portable, introducing millions to Tetris and Pokémon. Its simple design made it one of Nintendo’s most beloved systems.

Nintendo DS: Dual-Screen Innovation

Released in 2004, the DS became the best-selling handheld ever, thanks to its touchscreen gameplay and global hits like Nintendogs and Brain Age.

3DS: Glasses-Free 3D

The Nintendo 3DS carried forward handheld success in 2011, offering 3D visuals and classics like Animal Crossing: New Leaf and Fire Emblem Awakening.

Nintendo Switch: Hybrid Revolution

The 2017 Switch blurred the lines between handheld and home console, quickly becoming a cultural phenomenon with games like Breath of the Wild and Animal Crossing: New Horizons.

Game & Watch: Nintendo’s First Portable

Before the Game Boy, Nintendo’s Game & Watch series (1980) introduced handheld fun with single-screen games, shaping the company’s handheld future.

Virtual Boy: Nintendo’s Bold Experiment

Released in 1995, the Virtual Boy attempted 3D visuals years ahead of its time. Though short-lived, it remains a fascinating chapter in Nintendo’s hardware history.
